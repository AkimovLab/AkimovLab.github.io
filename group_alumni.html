Dr. Daeho Han

Daeho Han was born in Seoul, South Korea, in 1995. He earned his BS in Chemistry from Ajou University in 2017 and obtained his Ph.D in Chemistry from Ulsan National Institute of Science & Technology (UNIST). With guidance of Prof. Seung Kyu Min at UNIST, Daeho delved into nonadiabatic molecular dynamics (NA-MD) methods based on exact factorization (XF). He became one of developers of PyUNIxMD, a Python-based NA-MD software of the Min's group, and worked on developing new NA-MD methods tailored for extended and condensed systems, culminating in his thesis titled "Theoretical and Computational Description of Excited-state Phenomena in Condensed Phase." In October 2023, Daeho joined the Akimov group as a postdoctoral associate. First, he implemented and assessed trajectory-based NA-MD algorithms such as independent-trajectory XF methods (SHXF, MQCXF, EhXF), Quantum-Trajectory Surface Hopping (QTSH) and other internal algorithms, directly contributing to the Libra platform with his Python and C++ programming skills. Regarding the XF-based methods, he served as the main instructor at the Libra Workshop and Summer School in 2024. Furthermore, for simulating large-scale systems, he developed the Fully-Integrated Surface Hopping (FISH) method based on the neglect of backreaction approximation. Except for the trajectory-based algorithms, he also worked on the Local Diabatic Representation (LDR) dynamics method using the PyTorch features. Following his term at Buffalo, he has continued his career as a postdoctoral researcher at UNIST, appointed by the AI-CRED (AI Co-Research & Education for Innovative Drug) institute, S. Korea.
